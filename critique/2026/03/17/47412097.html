Summary

The article proposes classifying happiness as a psychiatric disorder, termed 'major affective disorder, pleasant type.' It argues that happiness is statistically abnormal, involves a distinct cluster of symptoms, is linked to cognitive abnormalities, and may reflect central nervous system dysfunction, dismissing objections based on the positive valuation of happiness.

Main Points & Arguments

  • Happiness is statistically abnormal compared to typical mental states.
  • Happiness consists of a discrete cluster of symptoms similar to psychiatric conditions.
  • Happiness is associated with cognitive abnormalities.
  • Happiness likely reflects abnormal central nervous system functioning.
  • The objection that happiness is not negatively valued is considered scientifically irrelevant.

Truthfulness Assessment

Questionable

The article presents a provocative and unconventional hypothesis rather than an established scientific consensus. While it cites relevant literature on affect and psychiatric classification, the proposal to classify happiness as a disorder is not supported by mainstream psychiatry or diagnostic manuals such as the DSM or ICD.
