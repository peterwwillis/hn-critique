It explains that while individual interceptor reliability (SSPK) and multiple interceptor assignments improve kill probability, real-world factors like tracking reliability and decoys complicate defense effectiveness significantly.

Main Points & Arguments

  • Single Shot Probability of Kill (SSPK) for interceptors like the U.S. GMD system is about 56%, requiring multiple interceptors per warhead to achieve high kill probabilities.
  • The overall kill probability depends heavily on successful detection and tracking (P(track)), which is often less than perfect and can be degraded by enemy actions.
  • The missile defense allocation problem is formally known as the Weapon-Target Assignment problem, which is NP-complete, making optimal interceptor assignment computationally challenging.
  • Decoys and classification errors increase the effective number of targets, further complicating defense allocation.
  • Recent advances in algorithms can solve large WTA instances efficiently, but the main challenge remains uncertainty in inputs and adversary advantages.
  • Current U.S. GMD interceptor inventory is limited and designed for a limited threat, with thin margins against larger or more sophisticated attacks.
  • Missile defense effectiveness is limited by both technical and operational factors, including software bugs, sensor destruction, and adversary tactics.
