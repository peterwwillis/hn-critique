Summary

The article explores the Repeat Your Self (RYS) technique of duplicating transformer layers in large language models (LLMs) to enhance performance without retraining. Using extensive experiments on the Qwen3.5-27B model, the author demonstrates that duplicating mid-stack layers improves reasoning capabilities and confirms the presence of discrete functional circuits in transformer architectures.

Main Points & Arguments

  • Duplicating middle transformer layers (RYS) in Qwen3.5-27B improves performance on math and emotional intelligence benchmarks.
  • The transformer stack has a three-phase structure: encoding, reasoning in a format-agnostic space, and decoding.
  • Mid-stack layers correspond to the reasoning phase and are the best candidates for duplication to boost model capabilities.
  • Single-layer repeats can provide some gains, but multi-layer contiguous blocks are more efficient and effective.
  • Beam search and surrogate modeling were used to explore millions of layer duplication configurations, finding that simple contiguous blocks dominate the efficiency frontier.
  • The RYS method is general across different transformer models and is orthogonal to fine-tuning, quantization, or prompt engineering.
  • The author released code, datasets, and RYS model variants on HuggingFace for community use.
