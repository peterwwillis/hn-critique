Main Points & Arguments

  • An ant mill occurs when army ants lose the pheromone trail and follow each other in a continuous loop.
  • This behavior is called a 'death spiral' because ants may die of exhaustion.
  • The phenomenon results from the self-organizing nature of ant colonies, where each ant follows the one in front.
  • The first documented observation was by William Beebe in 1921, describing a mill 370 meters in circumference.
  • Similar circular following behaviors have been observed in other species like processionary caterpillars and fish.
