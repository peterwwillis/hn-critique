Summary

Cornell researchers have developed a high-resolution 3D electron microscopy technique to visualize atomic-scale defects, termed 'mouse bites,' in semiconductor transistors. This advancement, achieved in collaboration with TSMC and ASM, allows unprecedented characterization of transistor channels, which is crucial for improving chip performance and debugging next-generation electronics.
