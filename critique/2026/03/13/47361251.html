Summary

The article describes a Chrome extension called Speech Speed that dynamically adjusts video playback speed based on the detected syllable rate of the speaker. It uses audio processing techniques to estimate the natural speaking rate and modifies playback speed to normalize speech to a comfortable listening rate.

Main Points & Arguments

  • The extension captures audio from the largest video element on a webpage using captureStream() and processes it with the Web Audio API.
  • It detects syllable rate by analyzing modulation in the energy envelope of the audio, using a zero-crossing count of a filtered RMS energy signal.
  • Playback speed is adjusted based on the ratio of a target syllable rate to the detected natural syllable rate, with smoothing to avoid abrupt changes.
  • The extension includes configurable parameters such as target syllable rate, minimum and maximum playback speeds, and provides a diagnostic overlay for monitoring.
  • Known limitations include incompatibility with DRM-protected content, potential false detections from music or sound effects, and challenges with multiple simultaneous speakers.
