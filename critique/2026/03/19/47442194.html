Summary

The article discusses the implementation of a vectorizer for Verilog designs to optimize verification and synthesis processes. The vectorizer reduces symbolic complexity in formal verification tools, improving performance metrics such as elaboration time and memory consumption.

Main Points & Arguments

  • Vectorization replaces multiple scalar operations with a single vector operation, common in traditional compilers but rare in Verilog.
  • Verilog supports vector notation but lacks semantic guarantees for vectorized signals, leading to similar hardware implementations for vectorized and scalar assignments.
  • Vectorization reduces symbolic complexity, benefiting formal verification tools like Cadence Jasper by treating buses as single symbolic entities.
  • The authors developed a Verilog vectorizer using the CIRCT infrastructure that recognizes various vectorization patterns.
  • Experiments with EDA tools showed a 28.12% improvement in elaboration time and a 51.30% reduction in memory consumption on over 1,100 designs from the ChiBench collection.
