Connecticut and the 1 Kilometer Effect (alearningaday.blog)

Summary

The article discusses a 2015 study by two geographers in Connecticut who found that the presence of solar panels on nearby houses was the strongest predictor of whether a homeowner would install solar panels themselves. This '1 kilometer effect' was later confirmed in studies from Sweden, China, and Germany, showing that proximity influences adoption of solar technology and reflecting a broader social phenomenon where people tend to adopt behaviors similar to those around them.

Main Points & Arguments

  • Early adopters of solar panels are influenced by interest in technology, trusted installers, and perceived benefits.
  • The biggest predictor for solar panel adoption is having neighbors within one kilometer who already have solar panels.
  • This proximity effect has been replicated in multiple countries including Sweden, China, and Germany.
  • The phenomenon illustrates a broader social principle where people tend to adopt behaviors prevalent in their social or physical proximity.
