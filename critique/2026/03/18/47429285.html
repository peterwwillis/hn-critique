  • Early adopters of white coats in surgery faced ridicule but gained patient trust due to the visible cleanliness of their attire.
  • Laboratory scientists adopted the white coat later, influenced by the growing relationship between clinical medicine and laboratory science in the early 20th century.
  • The white lab coat serves as a symbol of identity and professionalism, shaping perceptions and behavior, a phenomenon supported by psychological studies.
