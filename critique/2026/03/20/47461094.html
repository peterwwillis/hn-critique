Summary

The article describes how rewriting a Rust-based WASM parser in TypeScript resulted in a 2-4.6x speed improvement due to eliminating the costly WASM-JS boundary overhead and improving the streaming parsing algorithm from O(N²) to O(N). It explains that the main bottleneck was not Rust's parsing speed but the data transfer and serialization costs between WASM and JavaScript.

Main Points & Arguments

  • The original Rust WASM parser suffered from significant overhead due to copying and serializing data across the WASM-JS boundary.
  • Attempting to skip JSON serialization by returning JS objects directly from WASM was slower due to many fine-grained conversions.
  • Porting the parser fully to TypeScript eliminated the boundary overhead and improved per-call parsing speed by 2.2-4.6x.
  • The streaming parser was optimized by caching completed statement ASTs, reducing complexity from O(N²) to O(N) and improving total streaming parse time by 2.6-3.3x.
  • WASM is more suitable for compute-bound tasks with minimal interop, not for frequently called small-input parsing tasks due to boundary crossing costs.
  • Algorithmic improvements in parsing complexity had a larger impact on performance than the choice of implementation language.
