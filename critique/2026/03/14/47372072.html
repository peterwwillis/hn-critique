Main Points & Arguments

  • Traditional push-based streaming protocols like WebRTC, SRT, RTSP, and RTMP are inefficient for bandwidth-constrained environments because they constantly transmit all data.
  • MoQ uses a pull-based approach where viewers explicitly subscribe to specific media tracks, allowing selective transmission and better bandwidth management.
  • MoQ supports prioritization of streams, enabling important feeds (e.g., an AI-detected anomaly camera) to be transmitted first during bandwidth congestion.
  • Unlike some protocols that drop frames under congestion, MoQ queues and deprioritizes frames to allow backfilling for higher latency viewers like VOD services.
