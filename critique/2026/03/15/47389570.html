Summary

The article discusses the etiquette issues surrounding the uncritical sharing of AI-generated text, termed 'sloppypasta,' in professional communication. It highlights how forwarding raw AI output without verification or contextualization imposes additional cognitive burdens on recipients, erodes trust, and damages the sender's credibility. The article offers guidelines for responsible AI use, emphasizing reading, verifying, distilling, disclosing, and sharing AI-generated content thoughtfully.

Main Points & Arguments

  • Sharing unvetted AI-generated text (sloppypasta) in conversations creates an asymmetry of effort, shifting the burden of verification and comprehension onto recipients.
  • Raw AI output often lacks relevance, specificity, and may contain hallucinated or outdated information, making it unreliable without human review.
  • Using AI as a ghostwriter without disclosure can damage the sender's credibility if the content is incorrect or misleading.
  • The traditional balance of effort between writing and reading is disrupted by AI, increasing cognitive debt and reducing comprehension.
  • Trust in communication is undermined because recipients cannot easily assess the sender's expertise or the accuracy of AI-generated content.
  • Guidelines to mitigate these issues include reading and verifying AI output before sharing, distilling it to relevant points, disclosing AI assistance, sharing only when requested, and using links instead of large inline pastes.
