Summary

Bill C-22, titled the Lawful Access Act, 2026, proposes amendments to Canadian law to facilitate timely access to telecommunications data and subscriber information by law enforcement and intelligence agencies. The bill outlines procedures for obtaining warrants, production orders, and authorizations to access computer data, transmission data, and subscriber information, including from foreign entities, while also establishing obligations for electronic service providers.

Main Points & Arguments

  • The bill amends the Criminal Code and other acts to allow peace officers and public officers to obtain subscriber information, transmission data, and computer data more efficiently during investigations.

  • The bill authorizes requests to foreign telecommunications providers for transmission data or subscriber information relevant to investigations.
  • It enacts the Supporting Authorized Access to Information Act, which sets a framework for electronic service providers to assist authorized persons in accessing information.
  • The bill includes safeguards such as judicial oversight, conditions on warrants, and provisions for service providers to challenge demands or orders.
  • It prohibits disclosure of certain demands or orders for up to one year to protect investigations and establishes penalties for non-compliance.
