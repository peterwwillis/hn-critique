Summary

The article discusses the performance of Python compared to C, exploring the reasons behind Python's slower execution speed and presenting various optimization strategies. It emphasizes that while Python is inherently slower due to its dynamic design, there are ways to improve performance through upgrades and alternative runtimes.

Main Points & Arguments

  • Python is significantly slower than C in computational benchmarks, with ratios showing up to 875x slower for certain tasks.
  • The design choices in Python, such as dynamic typing and the Global Interpreter Lock (GIL), contribute to its performance issues.
  • Upgrading to newer versions of CPython can yield performance improvements, with the Faster CPython project introducing optimizations.
  • Alternative runtimes like PyPy and GraalPy can offer substantial speedups over CPython.
