Python: The Optimization Ladder (cemrehancavdar.com)

219 points by Twirrim 4 days ago  | 76 comments critique  | original on HN

Summary

The article discusses the performance of Python compared to C, emphasizing that while Python is inherently slower due to its dynamic nature, there are ways to optimize its performance. It presents various benchmarks and optimization strategies, illustrating the trade-offs involved in improving Python's execution speed.

Main Points & Arguments

  • Python is significantly slower than C, with benchmarks showing ratios of up to 875x.
  • The slow performance is attributed to Python's dynamic design, which requires runtime dispatch for operations.
  • Upgrading to newer versions of CPython can yield performance improvements, with CPython 3.11 showing notable speedups.
  • Alternative runtimes like PyPy and GraalPy can provide substantial performance gains over CPython.

Truthfulness Assessment

Reliable

The article accurately presents benchmarks comparing Python and C, and it correctly identifies the reasons for Python's slower performance. The claims regarding the impact of the GIL, the benefits of upgrading CPython, and the potential of alternative runtimes are well-supported by current knowledge in the Python community. However, some claims about the experimental features in CPython 3.13 and 3.14 should be verified against the latest developments in those versions.

Additional Considerations

  • The article does not discuss the specific use cases where Python's speed is less of a concern, such as in data analysis or web development.
  • It overlooks the importance of developer productivity and ease of use, which are often prioritized over raw performance.
